# Question Bank

Use these prompts to run a collaborative interview. Ask only 2-4 questions per turn.

## 1) Story seed

- What happened that made you want to write this now?
- Who is this for, and what should they think/do differently after reading?
- If readers only remember one point, what should it be?

## 2) Concrete timeline

- What was the starting state (team/system/process) before the problem?
- What was the first moment you realized this was bigger than expected?
- What happened next, step by step?

## 3) Decisions and tradeoffs

- What options did you seriously consider?
- Why did you choose this path over the alternatives?
- What did this decision optimize for, and what did it sacrifice?

## 4) Receipts (evidence)

- Which PRs/issues/commits/docs can we cite?
- What metrics changed (latency, error rate, deploy time, incidents, etc.)?
- What concrete example best proves the point?

## 5) Voice capture

- How would you explain this to a teammate in Slack in 4-5 sentences?
- Which sentence from your own words feels most "you"?
- What tone do you want: straight technical, reflective, lightly playful?

## 6) Closing move

- What should readers do next (specific action)?
- What would you do differently if you repeated this project?
- What warning would you give someone trying this approach?
