## Borrowable signals

- Open with a real moment instead of a generic thesis.
- Bring in specific details early (exact incidents, constraints, concrete artifacts).
- Let personality show up in short asides, not entire comedic bits.
- Keep momentum by preserving timeline and causality.
- Mix sentence lengths for rhythm.

## Red lines (do not cross)

- Do not copy signature phrasing, jokes, or repeated motifs from source authors.
- Do not force humor when the story is primarily technical.
- Do not trade precision for vibe.
- Do not turn every paragraph into a punchline setup.

## Practical blend

Default blend for Luan:
- 80% direct technical clarity (Luan baseline)
- 20% narrative flavor (Cabel-inspired techniques)

Increase narrative flavor only when user asks.

## Quick self-check before finalizing

- Could this have been written by Luan, based on his natural wording?
- Are there concrete receipts for major technical claims?
- Is there at least one memorable specific detail?
- Is humor present only where it feels natural?
